# Theo Feels Everything
### A Theodore's World Sensory Board Book

**Series:** Theodore's World — Book One
**Target Age:** 2–6 years old
**Format:** Board book, 9" x 9" square, 32 pages (16 spreads)
**Physical Sensory Elements:** Noted per spread (for print production)
**Author:** Anthony Marcano
**Characters:** Theo (age 5 in this book), Flutter the butterfly

---

## FRONT COVER

**Text:**
> *Theo Feels Everything*
> *And That Is His Superpower.*

**Illustration Brief:**
Theo — small, warm brown skin, thick curly dark hair, large expressive dark green eyes, round cheeks, small button nose, wearing his lavender T-shirt (puzzle piece drawn on chest in fabric marker) and mismatched sneakers (one red, one blue, velcro straps). He stands in a sun-drenched field of wildflowers. Eyes wide open, arms slightly spread at his sides, face full of pure wonder. Flutter the butterfly — gold/amber/lavender wings — hovers just above his right shoulder, wings catching the light. The sky is soft golden-hour light. The title text is bold, warm, playful.

**Physical Sensory Element:**
Flutter's wings on the cover have **holographic foil** — shimmers when tilted in light.

**Text:**

Theo sitting cross-legged in grass, face tilted up toward the sun, eyes gently closed, a small peaceful smile. Rays of golden light fall across his face and hands. Flutter rests lightly on his knee. ## SPREAD 3 — PAGES 6–7

**Text:**
> *Some things are too loud.*
> *CRASH. BANG. BEEP BEEP BEEP.*
> *Theo's ears feel too full.*

**Illustration Brief:**
A busy street scene behind Theo — cars honking, a jackhammer, a dog barking, a bus rumbling. Theo is in the foreground with his navy noise-canceling headphones pressed over his ears, eyes squeezed shut, shoulders up near his ears. Sound waves are illustrated as jagged red/orange shapes filling the air around him. Flutter hovers close, wings lowered in concern.

**Design Note:** The sound words (CRASH, BANG, BEEP BEEP BEEP) are illustrated in BIG, bold, wobbly font — chaotic.

**Physical Sensory Element:**
**Press-sound button** embedded in this spread — plays a gentle version of ambient street noise (soft, not overwhelming), so children and caregivers can experience and discuss it together.

## SPREAD 6 — PAGES 12–13

**Text:**
> *Theo feels things very, very deeply.*
> *When he is happy — he is SO happy.*
> *His hands go flutter, flutter, flutter.*

**Illustration Brief:**
Theo in full happy stim — hands at his sides, flapping with pure joy, up on his toes, eyes wide and bright with delight. He's looking at something wonderful just off-panel (let the child imagine what it is). Gold sparks fly from his flapping hands. Flutter mirrors him — wings beating fast, excited little zigzag flight path, turquoise eyes bright. The background bursts with warm confetti-like shapes in all rainbow colors.

**Text Design Note:** The word "flutter, flutter, flutter" in the text is in a lighter, italic font — it should feel gentle and rhythmic when read aloud.

**Physical Sensory Element:**
The hands in the illustration have a **soft, velvety texture patch** — smooth and comforting to touch.

**Text:**
> *Some textures feel WRONG.*
> *Scratchy tags. Stiff seams. Tight waistbands.*
> *Theo's skin says: NO THANK YOU.*

**Illustration Brief:**
Theo sitting on his bed, pulling at the back collar of a shirt, face scrunched in clear discomfort. The offending shirt is illustrated with visual "prickle" indicators around the seams. His mismatched sneakers are lined up neatly by the bed — already off. Flutter hovers nearby with an empathetic expression — antenna tips glowing amber (the "concern" color). The bedroom is cozy and Theo-ish — soft colors, stuffed animals, his backpack on a hook.

**Design Note:** The "scratchy" elements (tags, seams) are shown in a slightly exaggerated, almost comedic way — relatable but not distressing.

## SPREAD 11 — PAGES 22–23

**Text:**
> *Flutter feels things too.*
> *Happy: wings glow brighter.*
> *Alert: wings go deep amber — like a small, brave fire.*

**Illustration Brief:**
Two side-by-side panels of Flutter.
LEFT: Flutter glowing bright gold-yellow, wings fully spread, sparkling, turquoise eyes wide with joy, antenna tips golden.
RIGHT: Flutter in alert mode — wings deeper amber-orange, antenna pointed forward like radar, eyes focused and determined, posture tense but brave.

**Design Note:** Both panels have the same soft white background so the color shift of the wings is dramatic and clear. Children can compare and discuss.

**Physical Sensory Element:**
**Color-change ink** on Flutter's wings — when a child touches with warm fingers, the wings shift from gold to amber. (Heat-reactive thermochromic ink.)

| 15 | Raised gold foil — infinity symbol | Specialty print |

### Read-Aloud Notes:
- Read slowly. This book is made for pausing.
- On Spread 3–4: pause after the sound button. Ask the child: "Does that feel like too much? Or just right?"
- On Spread 5: let the child flap their hands with Theo.
- On Spread 6: encourage hand-flapping together — celebrate it.
- On Spread 12: no need to explain. Just sit with it.
- On Spread 15: whisper "I am enough" together.

### Companion Activities (for website / educator guide):
- Theo's Sensory Map: children draw/mark what feels good vs. what is too much
- Flutter's Color Feelings: match emotions to Flutter's wing colors
- My Calm Kit: children identify their own calm tools (like Theo's worry stone, headphones)
- "I am enough" mirror card: printable affirmation card for child's mirror
